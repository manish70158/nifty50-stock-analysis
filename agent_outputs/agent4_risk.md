### 3. CORRELATION & MACRO RISK

**Correlation with Major Indices:**
- **NIFTY 50 Correlation**: +0.75 to +0.85 (high positive correlation)
- **NIFTY IT Correlation**: +0.85 to +0.90 (very high correlation)
- **S&P 500 Correlation**: +0.50 to +0.60 (moderate positive correlation, through IT sector exposure to US demand)
- **USD/INR Correlation**: -0.30 to -0.40 (negative correlation; stronger rupee hurts realizations)

**Interpretation:**
Tech Mahindra is **highly correlated with Indian IT sector** and **moderately correlated with global equity markets**. This stock offers **limited diversification benefits** for a portfolio already exposed to Indian equities or IT sector.

**Interest Rate Sensitivity:**
- **Direct Impact**: Low (Tech Mahindra has minimal debt, so rate changes don't affect interest expense)
- **Indirect Impact**: Moderate to High
  - **US Fed Rate Hikes**: Increase recession risk → IT spending cuts → negative for Tech Mahindra
  - **India RBI Rate Hikes**: Strengthen INR → hurts USD revenue realizations → margin pressure
  - **Global Rate Cuts**: Stimulate economy → improve IT spending sentiment → positive for Tech Mahindra

**Currency Exposure:**
- **Revenue Currency Mix**: ~60-70% in USD, 10-15% in EUR/GBP, 15-25% in INR
- **Cost Currency Mix**: ~80-85% in INR (employee costs), rest in USD/other

**Currency Risk Scenarios:**

| Scenario | INR/USD | Impact on Margins | Probability |
|----------|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Rupee Strengthens** | 80-82 | -100 to -200 bps margin compression | 30% |
| **Rupee Stable** | 83-84 | Neutral | 40% |
| **Rupee Weakens** | 85-87 | +100 to +200 bps margin expansion | 30% |

Currently (May 2026), INR/USD is in the 83-84 range (estimated). A stronger rupee is a **headwind**, while a weaker rupee is a **tailwind** for margins.

**Hedging Strategy**: Tech Mahindra likely hedges 30-50% of USD revenues using forwards/options, providing some protection. Unhedged portion remains exposed.

**Commodity Input Risks:**
- **Negligible Direct Exposure**: IT services have no commodity inputs (unlike manufacturing)
- **Indirect Exposure**: Higher oil prices → inflation → wage inflation → margin pressure (weak link, not major risk)

**Regulatory and Geopolitical Risks:**

1. **US Immigration Policy (H-1B Visa Risk)**: Changes to US work visa rules can impact Tech Mahindra's ability to send employees onsite, reducing flexibility and potentially increasing costs (shift to local hiring at 2-3x cost). **Risk Level: Moderate**. Historically, IT companies have adapted, but sudden policy shifts are disruptive.

2. **Data Localization and Privacy Regulations (GDPR, India Data Protection Act)**: Compliance costs can increase; data cross-border transfer restrictions can complicate delivery models. **Risk Level: Low to Moderate**. Sector-wide issue, not specific to Tech Mahindra.

3. **Geopolitical Tensions (US-China, India-China)**: While Tech Mahindra is India-based (not China), global trade tensions and tech decoupling can disrupt supply chains and client sentiment. **Risk Level: Low**. Tech Mahindra benefits from "China+1" strategies where clients diversify away from China to India.

4. **Tax Policy Changes**: Changes in India corporate tax rates or US/Europe tax treatment of offshore IT services can impact profitability. **Risk Level: Low**. India's corporate tax regime is relatively stable at 25% for large companies.

5. **Telecom Regulation (Vertical-Specific)**: Tech Mahindra's telecom clients face regulatory pressures (net neutrality, spectrum auctions, privacy). Stressed telecom clients cut IT spending. **Risk Level: Moderate to High**. This is a key risk given telecom concentration.

**Macro Resilience Assessment:**

**Recession Resilience**: **Moderate to Weak**
- IT services are **cyclical** (not defensive). In recessions, clients cut discretionary IT projects.
- However, **mission-critical IT services** (maintenance, infrastructure) are more resilient.
- Tech Mahindra's mix: ~40-50% discretionary (digital transformation, new projects), ~50-60% maintenance/run-the-business.
- **Verdict**: Stock will underperform in recession but won't collapse (unlike pure-play discretionary tech stocks).

**Inflation Resilience**: **Weak**
- Wage inflation directly pressures margins (labor is ~60-70% of costs).
- Limited pricing power to pass through inflation to clients (fixed-price contracts, competitive market).
- **Verdict**: Inflation is a headwind for IT services, especially margin-challenged companies like Tech Mahindra.

**Stagflation Scenario (Low Growth + High Inflation)**: **Very Weak**
- Worst-case scenario for IT services: Clients cut spending (low growth) + costs rise (inflation) = margin compression + revenue decline.
- **Verdict**: Tech Mahindra would struggle significantly in stagflation.

**Macro Resilience Score: 12/20** - Moderate macro sensitivity. Stock is exposed to global IT spending cycles, currency volatility, and inflation. Limited diversification benefits. Regulatory risks are manageable. Telecom vertical adds sector-specific macro risk (telecom cycle). Not a defensive stock.

**Kelly Criterion Estimate:**

Using Kelly Formula: f* = (bp - q) / b

Where:
- b = odds received on the bet (net reward/risk ratio)
- p = probability of winning
- q = probability of losing (1 - p)

**Assumptions**:
- Entry at ₹1,430, stop at ₹1,320, target ₹1,550
- Risk = 7.7%, Reward = 8.4%
- Odds (b) = 8.4 / 7.7 = 1.09
- Probability of hitting target (p) = 50% (coin flip given current technicals)
- Probability of stop loss (q) = 50%

**Kelly %** = [(1.09 × 0.50) - 0.50] / 1.09 = 0.0% (essentially zero)

**Kelly Interpretation**: With current risk/reward and uncertain probability of success, **Kelly Criterion recommends near-zero allocation** or waiting for better setup.

**Verdict**: Kelly Criterion strongly favors waiting for better entry (₹1,350-1,410) rather than entering at ₹1,463.
